Considerations
Briefing and debriefing are fundamental elements of our search practice.

Never underestimate the importance of your notes, your sketches and your observations.

It’s better to record and not need it than regret not taking notes, especially several months down the line if required to give evidence in a Coroner’s Court.

We have all got pieces of the jigsaw and when we combine these pieces we start to complete the jigsaw, which in turn shows the bigger picture of the situation.
